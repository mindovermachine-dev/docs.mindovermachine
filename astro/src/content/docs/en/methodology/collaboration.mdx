## Purpose

This page describes how organizations can collaborate with the foundation through membership, contributions, and projects.

The goal is to create a transparent model where partners can support the foundation's long-term mission while receiving concrete value through ongoing dialogue and development.

## What Membership Is

Membership is a business model for collaboration with the foundation.

It provides access to structured collaboration, professional sparring, shared learning, and prioritized access to laboratory capacity.

Membership also helps fund shared infrastructure for method development, knowledge sharing, and the Community of Practice.

## What Membership Is Not

Membership does not grant ownership of the foundation.

Membership does not grant voting rights in foundation governance or the right to instruct the board.

Membership does not automatically grant a seat in the Representative Council.

Governance rights are defined by statutes and governance documents, not by commercial agreements.

## Collaboration Modes

Collaboration can happen in three tracks.

1. Membership with recurring annual fee and access to shared activities.
2. Project agreements with defined scope, timeline, and deliverables.
3. Combined tracks where membership and projects reinforce each other.

Projects are always priced separately. Membership can improve access, startup speed, and continuity, but it does not replace a project agreement.

## Indicative Membership Tiers

These tiers are a first draft inspired by pricing patterns in comparable Nordic non-profit networks, professional communities, and foundation partnership models.

All prices are per year, excl. VAT.

### Base

12,000 DKK

Suitable for smaller organizations, startups, and public institutions with limited budgets.

### Partner

36,000 DKK

Suitable for organizations with active development needs and closer collaboration ambitions.

### Strategic Partner

96,000 DKK

Suitable for organizations aiming for multi-year collaboration and high shared ambition.

## Projects and Commercial Terms

Project work is contracted and priced separately.

Membership may provide better onboarding or capacity conditions, but does not imply automatic discounting on all project work.

The concrete model is managed by executive leadership within board-defined principles.

## Roles and Accountability

The board sets the overarching principles for ethics, conflicts of interest, transparency, and risk boundaries.

Executive leadership defines commercial terms, pricing points, contracts, and capacity allocation.

Executive leadership reports at least annually to the board on model performance, impact, risks, and adjustments.

## Annual Review and Transparency

The model is reviewed at least annually as part of the annual cycle.

The foundation publishes overarching membership principles while contract-specific and commercially sensitive details remain confidential.

Pricing and package details can be adjusted to keep the model fair, sustainable, and aligned with the foundation mission.
